# Navide Marketing Prototype Validation Guide

## Objective

Test whether agent-heavy solo engineers understand Navide's category, audience, concrete difference, and current maturity before investing in a production marketing site.

This is a comprehension study, not a request for compliments.

## Participant profile

Recruit five people who:

- Build or maintain software personally
- Use at least one coding agent several times per week
- Have managed more than one agent session or terminal context
- Are not already deeply familiar with Navide's internal language

Do not count the founder or contributors who helped write the positioning.

## Five-second test

1. Show only the hero area for five seconds.
2. Hide it.
3. Ask these questions without prompting:

   - What do you think this product is?
   - Who is it for?
   - What problem does it solve?
   - What phrase or image do you remember?

Record exact language. Do not explain Navide until the participant finishes answering.

### Pass threshold

At least four of five participants should identify:

- A software engineering or development environment
- One engineer or a small operator as the first audience
- Coordination or direction of multiple AI agents as a central distinction

## Workflow comprehension test

1. Show the 45-60 second real-product demo without narration.
2. Ask:

   - What did the engineer ask the system to do?
   - What work happened in parallel?
   - Why did the engineer need to intervene?
   - What evidence showed the work was ready?
   - How is this different from using an AI chat inside an editor?

### Pass threshold

At least four of five participants can name one concrete product difference and correctly identify the human exception.

## Maturity comprehension test

Show the `Available today` and `Product direction` section. Ask:

- What can you use now?
- What is still a future direction?
- How would you install Navide today?
- What security or privacy boundary would you want to investigate?

No participant should conclude that a signed public download, complete sandbox, universal offline mode, or comprehensive autonomous policy engine already exists.

## Action test

Ask the participant to choose one next action without suggesting an answer:

- Watch the longer product tour
- Visit GitHub
- Build from source
- Join early access, if a real intake path exists
- Do nothing

Then ask why. Intent and reasoning are more useful than raw click counts at this stage.

## Decision rules

### Expand into a production site

Proceed when the category, audience, concrete difference, and maturity boundaries pass their thresholds, and at least two participants express a credible high-intent next action.

### Refine the message

Revise the hero and comparison when people understand the product after the demo but not within the initial five seconds.

### Improve product evidence

Revise the demo when people remember the vision but cannot name a visible workflow difference.

### Prioritize product readiness

Pause marketing expansion when high-intent participants are blocked primarily by distribution, onboarding, reliability, safety, or missing core workflow capability.
